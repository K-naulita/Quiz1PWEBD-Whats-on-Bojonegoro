East Java, Indonesia

BOJONEGORO

A small district that borders East Java and Central Java. They say there’s a lot of history buried here.

Oil City

It is one of the oil producing areas, so that there are oil companies established here, including ExxonMobil.

Passed by Bengawan Solo

This city has many ancient fossil discoveries near this river.

Bojonegoro has no sea and no mountains, which means its challenges do not come from tsunamis or earthquakes, but from floods. The most devastating flood struck in 2005, when the Bengawan Solo river overflowed and paralyzed much of the region. Yet, this same river is also a source of pride, celebrated every year through the Bengawan Festival, a vibrant cultural event where colorful costumes and creativity bring the riverbanks to life.

Bengawan Solo

It is the longest river on the island of Java. It flows through the city and provides a livelihood for the surrounding community.

Because the Bengawan Solo River, the land has long supported rich ecosystems and proof of this is found in the many fossils unearthed here. Excavations have revealed remains of giant Stegodon elephants, ancient buffalo, banteng, and even a three-meter fossil of a prehistoric whale. Showing that the region once hosted both land and sea life.

Over millions of years, the same layers of earth that preserved these fossils also trapped rich deposits of oil and gas. Today, Bojonegoro is home to the Cepu Block and the Banyu Urip oil field, among Indonesia’s largest, managed by ExxonMobil and Pertamina, making energy production a new livelihood for the region.
